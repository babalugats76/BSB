History

Started in 1993, the Backstreet Boys quickly rose to fame, from a small group of Orlando youngsters who could sing and dance, to a team of musical superstars with eight albums, two movies, eleven world tours, and countless awards between them.

As the years passed, the group has been responsive to the changing demands of their adoring audiences. Evolving styles have taken the BSB from R&B and club to hip-hop and pop. With time, the group's impending maturity became evident, however, with their abandonment of synthesizers in favor of live instruments. As do many things, however, the BSB have recently come full-circle and are embracing again the dance-beat energy with a distinctly electropop feel.

Over the years, the "boys" have come to be great businessmen. After years of fighting their original manager, Lou Pearlman, who took advantage of their youth and business naivete, they now work in partnership with a number of large corporations; recently, Sears, Old Navy, and Burger King have all benefitted from this group's larger than life presence in popular - and commercial - culture.

Did You Know?

  • Kevin and Brian are actually cousins, growing up close to each other in Lexington, Kentucky.
  • Nick is deeply passionate about the health of our planet and was selected as a U. N. Special Ambassador to help spread the word about environmental conservation and protection.
  • Before finding fame with the Backstreet Boys, Howie was professionally trained as a dancer, and even performed with an all-boy ballet troupe.
  • AJ is afraid of clowns and freaks out whenever he sees one; his mom protects him when they are on tour, however, as she is their tour publicist.
